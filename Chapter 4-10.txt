The White House on Wednesday night announced that Trump plans to cut the federal reserve system's retirement benefit by 20 percent and slash its payroll tax rate by 3.5 percent, its biggest spending cut in decades.

The policy change, which comes on the heels of Trump's announcement that he'd scrap his long-term plan to slash the federal government's public sector pension plan, is expected to send a strong signal to his predecessors that he's willing to pursue his agenda for the future.

The change is similar to what President Barack Obama did in the 1970s when he cut Social Security and Medicare benefits.

"It's a continuation of what he's done in his first term, and I think that is a good sign," said Jim Margolis, chief of staff to Trump's chief of staff from 2009 to 2012.
